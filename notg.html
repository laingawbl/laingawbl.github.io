element-to-element powers of two vectors

I am nervous about getting a co-op position this September. To reassure myself, I turned to plotting my chances in Octave. If I have, on average, a probability p of getting any one programming co-op, and I apply to n jobs, the probability I get a co-op for September is 

1 - ( (1-p) ^ n )

, so I thought I would do this over a linear space for p from 0.025 to 0.2 (varying degrees of confidence) and n from 1 to 51 (varying degrees of effort). However, as it so happens, you can't - at least, I could not find any way of doing this - take the element-to-elementwise power of two vectors (the linear spaces) like that. The solution was to write 

function p = vvpow(x, y)
	p = zeros(size(y, 2), size(x, 2));
	for i = [1:size(y, 2)]
		p(i, :) = (1-x).^y(i);
	end
end

And so, finally: 

>> t = linspace(0.025, 0.2, 101);
>> g = linspace(1, 51, 51);
>> mesh(t, g, vvpow(t, g));
>> zlabel('1 - p(job)');
>> ylabel('n. applied to');
>> xlabel('p(each job)');
